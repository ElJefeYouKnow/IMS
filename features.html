Current Release

Feature depth where operations teams actually need it.

The current Modulr release is Inventory Management System. It is built around the daily friction points: missing stock, unclear ownership, weak receiving processes, overdue returns, and reporting that arrives too late. The rest of the platform remains on the roadmap.

What teams can do right now inside Inventory Management System.

Project support Tie material movement to jobs, shortages, and outstanding returns.

Core Workflows

Everything built around movement, not just records.

Every feature is organized around operational events so the system reflects what really happened, who did it, and what action comes next.

Receiving and check-in

Capture inbound stock, tie it to orders, and update available inventory in real time.

Checkout and issue

Move stock to crews and projects with clear responsibility and return expectations.

Reservation and allocation

Reserve stock for future work and keep project demand visible before shortages hit the field.

Returns and exceptions

Track overdue returns, damage, loss, and discrepancy reasons in the same operating record.

Role-Based Experience

Different teams get different signal, without losing the shared truth.

  • 1Employees get fast action paths for receiving, issuing, and returning material.
  • 2Managers see fill rate, service pressure, count backlog, and overdue operational work.
  • 3Admins manage procurement suggestions, catalog quality, permissions, and audit exposure.

Control Layer

Catalog, procurement, and audit features that hold the system together.

Modulr is not just movement tracking. It is the control system around data quality, reorder logic, supplier coverage, and audit confidence.

Item governance

Keep categories, reorder points, low-stock rules, supplier links, and costing aligned.

Procurement visibility

Open orders, inbound ETAs, shortages, and receiving status stay visible without side spreadsheets.

Cycle count focus

See which items are overdue for counting and where inventory accuracy is starting to drift.

Audit-ready history

Every adjustment, loss, damage event, and return stays traceable by role, time, and reason.
